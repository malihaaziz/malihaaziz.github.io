About Me

I'm a highly analytical and vision-driven computational biologist with over a decade of experience applying AI/ML and multi-omics approaches to biomarker discovery, host-pathogen interactions, and translational research. Currently pursuing my PhD in Health Data Science at George Washington University.

At the Antibiotic Resistance Action Center, I build infrastructure, algorithms, and collaborative teams that drive innovation at scale. My work spans from developing diagnostic assays using machine learning to creating high-throughput genomic pipelines that have real-world clinical applications.

I'm passionate about bridging the gap between computational methods and translational medicine, with a particular focus on infectious disease research and antimicrobial resistance.
